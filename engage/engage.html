How to care for clothes

...because the washer and dryer just won't cut it

Wool

  • Hand wash is best, but machine washing on the cold, delicate cycle is okay.
  • Never put in the dryer!
  • Don't wring out water, press with an absorbant towel to dry.
  • Lay flat to dry.
  • Don't hang dry, this can cause the garment to become misshapen.

Down Puffers

  • Machine-washable on delicate or bulky setting in a FRONT LOADING machine
  • Machine dry on low heat with two to three tennis balls or dryer balls.
  • The addition of the balls helps restore the puffiness of the garment.

Fur

  • Hand wash only!
  • Don't wring out water, press with an absorbant towel.
  • Hang to let drip dry.
  • Once dry, use a bristled brush to detangle and smoothen the look of the fibers.

Mesh

  • Machine-washable on delicate cycle.
  • Let air dry. The holes in mesh make it easy to catch onto buttons or zippers in a dryer, increasing the likelihood of tears in the garment.

Studs

  • Hand wash is best.
  • Machine-washable in a mesh delicates bag to prevent abraisions that could loosen the embellishment.
  • Let hang to dry, but towel dry any metal embellishments, such as studs, as water exposure can cause rusting.
